"The Last Judgement", Italian: Il Giudizio Universale, by Michelangelo

Vatican Museums. Archived from the original on 29 July 2016.

Central Figure of Christ:


At the heart of the composition is Christ, depicted as a powerful and authoritative figure, radiating divine judgment. His raised right hand signifies condemnation, casting the damned into Hell, while his lowered left hand indicates mercy and salvation for the blessed. This dual gesture underscores the dichotomy of divine justice. Surrounding Christ are the Virgin Mary and a host of saints, reflecting their intercessory roles and emphasizing Christ's central role in Christian salvation history.

Dynamic and Chaotic Composition:


Michelangelo's use of a dynamic and swirling composition creates a sense of movement and turmoil. The saved, on the left side of Christ, are shown ascending towards Heaven, assisted by angels, symbolizing their salvation and eternal reward. Conversely, on the right, the damned are depicted being dragged down to Hell by demons, illustrating their eternal punishment. This chaotic arrangement serves to highlight the dramatic nature of the Last Judgment and the finality of each soul's fate.

Representation of Saints and Martyrs:


The fresco features numerous saints and martyrs who can be identified by their specific attributes and the instruments of their martyrdom. For instance, Saint Bartholomew holds his flayed skin, referencing his martyrdom, and Saint Catherine of Alexandria is shown with a wheel, the instrument of her torture. These figures serve to remind viewers of the trials and sacrifices of the saints, as well as their triumphant roles in the heavenly court, reinforcing the virtues of faith and endurance.

Depiction of Final Judgment:


"The Last Judgment" portrays the Christian eschatological belief in the final judgment of souls at the end of the world. According to this belief, Christ will return to judge the living and the dead. The fresco dramatically captures this moment, illustrating the ultimate fate of humanity, where souls are judged and separated into those who are saved and those who are damned.

Symbolism and Allegory:


"The Last Judgment" is replete with symbolic elements and allegorical figures. Angels and demons battle for souls, symbolizing the eternal struggle between good and evil. The resurrection of the dead at the bottom of the fresco illustrates the Christian belief in bodily resurrection, while Charon, the mythological ferryman of the dead from Dante's "Inferno," is included to draw a connection between Christian and classical themes of the afterlife. Additionally, the fresco reflects Michelangelo's deep understanding of human anatomy and emotion, using physical form and expression to convey complex theological ideas about sin, redemption, and divine justice.
